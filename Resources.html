Shooting Checklist

Step 1: Stance

Straddle the shooting line with one foot on either side.
Move the foot closest to the target back so that the toes of that foot line up with the center of the arch of the outside foot.
Angle the hips by turning the toes of both feet toward the target approximately 30 degrees.

Step 2: Nock

Ensure the index vane is pointing away out or away from the riser
And the arrow is snapped onto the string

Step 3: Hook and Grip

Ensure the grip on the bow is loose and the knuckles are at a 45 degree angle to the bow
Ensure your index, middle and ring fingertips are curled around the string below the nocking point

Step 4: Postue/ Alignment

Stance, hook and grip maintained.
Your head is turned toward the target.
The bow arm is out of the path of the string

Step 5: Raise Bow

The bow arm is lifted straignt up from the ground.
The bow hand is stopped at shoulder height.

Step 6: Draw

Pull the bowstring back in a straight line towards the face.

Step 7: Anchor

The index finger of the draw hand is settled in the corner of the mouth.

Step 8: Transfer to Hold

The draw arm elbow moves slightly behind the arrow,

Step 9: Aim

Place the point of the arrow toward the intended target.
The focus is equally on the arrow point and th target.

Step 10: Release/ Follow Through

Relax the fingertips and allow te string to roll off the fingers.
The shot is finished by moving the drawing arm so that the drawing hand stops between the ear and shoulder.

Step 11: Ask for Feedback!
